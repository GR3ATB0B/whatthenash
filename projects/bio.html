Who I Am

I'm Nash — an 18-year-old senior at Atlanta Classical Academy, heading to Auburn University for Engineering in Fall 2026. I'm a builder at heart: I design wireless systems with Arduino and ESP32s, 3D-print custom enclosures, experiment with Raspberry Pi, and I'm always learning CAD to bring ideas to life.

At the core of everything I do is my Christian faith. My projects, my leadership, and my drive all flow from that foundation — I believe in using my gifts to serve something greater than myself.

What I Do

I'm a supervisor at Chick-fil-A, where I use my Spanish daily with the kitchen staff. On the field, I play center on the football team — leading by example in the weight room and on game day. I'm also in choir and compete in engineering competitions.

How I'm Wired

I have ADHD and dyslexia — and they're not obstacles, they're part of what makes me relentless, resourceful, and wired to think differently. I've learned to turn limitations into leverage and curiosity into capability. I don't learn the "normal" way, and that's exactly why I solve problems the way I do.

When I'm Not Building

You'll find me brewing coffee, tinkering with electronics, flying RC aircraft, fishing, or building something with my grandfather. There's always a project on the bench.
